Title: First Aid for Minor Thermal Burns (Educational Summary)

A thermal burn is tissue injury caused by heat from flames, hot liquids, steam, or contact with hot objects. This summary covers minor burns suitable for basic first aid; large, deep, or complex burns require professional emergency care.

For minor superficial burns (often red, painful, without broken blisters over a small area): first, ensure the scene is safe and remove the person from the heat source. Cool the burn with cool (not ice-cold) running water for 10–20 minutes. Ice directly on skin is not recommended because it can worsen tissue damage. Remove tight jewelry or clothing near the burn only if it comes off easily without pulling stuck fabric.

After cooling, gently pat the area dry with a clean cloth. Cover with a sterile non-stick dressing or clean plastic wrap to reduce infection risk and pain from air exposure. Do not apply butter, toothpaste, or unverified home remedies. Over-the-counter pain relievers may help if the person has no contraindications and can use them safely.

Seek emergency medical care if: the burn is larger than the person's palm, affects the face, hands, feet, genitals, or major joints, appears white/charred/leathery (possible deep burn), was caused by chemicals or electricity, involves smoke inhalation, or shows signs of infection (increasing redness, swelling, pus, fever).

Chemical burns: brush off dry chemicals before rinsing (if safe), flush with large amounts of water, and follow product safety instructions when available. Electrical burns: ensure power is off before helping; even small entry wounds may hide internal injury—evaluation in a hospital is advised.

Prevention tips include using oven mitts, setting water heater temperatures to reduce scalding risk, and keeping hot liquids away from children. First aid training from certified organizations improves preparedness. This text is for learning purposes and does not replace certified first aid instruction or emergency services (call local emergency number when in doubt).
